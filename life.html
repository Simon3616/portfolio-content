Snowboard

On Christmas 1965, the Sherman Poppen family of Muskegon, Michigan, finished singing Christmas carols as the children unwrapped Santa's presents, snowboards! The two daughters were very happy. He took the children up the mountain to ski. Poppen had an idea, tied the two skis together and combined them into one, and then improved it. It was designed as a single-piece board that was shorter and wider than traditional skis, and it was equipped with ropes for steering. . The neighborhood kids are clamoring for this very fun new toy. In 1966, Sherman patented his idea for a surf-style ski. His wife suggested combining the English words "snow" and "surf," and Snurfer was born. Poppen licensed his invention to the Brunswick Corporation, and it became a hit, selling nearly a million copies by 1970.
